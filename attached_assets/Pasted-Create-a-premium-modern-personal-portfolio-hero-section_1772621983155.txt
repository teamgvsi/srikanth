Summary text

Srikanth Raheja moderated a candid discussion at CIE IIIT Hyderabad with industry leaders Sai Chaitanya Kokku (Director, Draper Startup House Hyderabad) and Sheetal Patil (CEO, Cream Stone Ice Cream Concepts). The session explored what separates fast-scaling startups from stable businesses and the strategic decisions founders must make to transition between the two.

Key Insights

• Startups are experiments, while businesses are repeatable systems built on validated unit economics.

• Incubators and accelerators help founders convert early hypotheses into scalable processes.

• For retail and consumer brands, operational excellence, supplier relationships, and margin architecture matter more than aggressive acquisition.

• Early teams should hire adaptable generalists before bringing in specialists after product–market fit.

• Fundraising should solve a specific bottleneck rather than become the primary goal.

Closing message

The session concluded with a call for founders to focus on the right growth levers—traction, operations, hiring, and funding—and continue building stronger entrepreneurial ecosystems through open conversations.
